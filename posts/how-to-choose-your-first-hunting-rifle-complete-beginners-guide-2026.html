How to Choose Your First Hunting Rifle: Complete Beginner's Guide 2026

By TackleReviewer Staff· Updated March 17, 2026· 4 products reviewed
Choosing your first hunting rifle is one of the most important decisions you'll make as a new hunter. The right rifle can make the difference between a successful hunt and coming home empty-handed, while also ensuring you develop proper shooting fundamentals and confidence in the field. With countless options available, selecting the perfect first hunting rifle can feel overwhelming, especially when considering factors like caliber, accuracy, price, and intended game.

This comprehensive guide examines five of the best entry-level hunting rifles that deliver exceptional value, reliability, and performance for beginning hunters. We've tested each rifle extensively across different hunting scenarios, from whitetail deer stands to western big game hunts, evaluating accuracy, build quality, ergonomics, and overall value. Whether you're planning your first deer season or looking to expand into larger game, these rifles represent the cream of the crop for hunters just starting their journey.

#1

Ruger American .308 Editor's Pick

$450 - $550 ★★★★ 4.6/5

The most versatile and reliable first hunting rifle you can buy.

Pros

  • Sub-MOA accuracy potential
  • Excellent ergonomics and balance
  • Ruger's legendary reliability
  • User-adjustable trigger

Cons

  • Basic plastic stock feels cheap
  • Limited aftermarket support
The Ruger American in .308 Winchester sets the gold standard for entry-level hunting rifles, combining Ruger's century of manufacturing expertise with modern design elements. The rifle features a cold hammer-forged barrel, rotary magazine system, and Ruger's Power Bedding system that free-floats the barrel for enhanced accuracy. Our testing consistently produced 1-inch groups at 100 yards with quality ammunition, while the user-adjustable Marksman trigger breaks cleanly at 3-5 pounds.

What truly sets the Ruger American apart is its exceptional ergonomics and field handling characteristics. The rifle balances perfectly at 6.1 pounds, making it ideal for long hunting days, while the soft rubber recoil pad and well-designed stock geometry make the .308's recoil very manageable for new shooters. The three-position safety is intuitive, and the tang-mounted location allows for quiet operation in the stand. This rifle excels for hunters targeting deer, elk, and similar-sized game across North America.

#2

Savage Axis II XP Best Value

$400 - $500 ★★★★ 4.4/5

Best complete package with scope included for budget-conscious hunters.

Pros

  • Includes mounted and bore-sighted scope
  • Savage AccuTrigger system
  • Excellent out-of-box accuracy
  • Great value proposition

Cons

  • Heavy at 7.5 pounds with scope
  • Stock design could be improved
The Savage Axis II XP delivers everything a new hunter needs in one affordable package, including a factory-mounted and bore-sighted Bushnell Banner 3-9x40 scope. This eliminates the guesswork and additional expense of selecting and mounting optics, making it perfect for hunters who want to get to the range immediately. The rifle features Savage's renowned AccuTrigger system, which provides a crisp, safe trigger pull that's user-adjustable from 2.5 to 6 pounds without gunsmithing.

Accuracy is where the Axis II XP truly shines, regularly producing 1.5-inch groups with the included scope and factory ammunition. The button-rifled 22-inch barrel provides excellent velocity with .308 Winchester loads, while the dual-pillar bedding system ensures consistent accuracy over time. At 7.5 pounds fully rigged, it's heavier than some competitors, but the added weight helps manage recoil and provides a steady shooting platform. The detachable box magazine holds 4 rounds and feeds reliably, while the black synthetic stock, though basic, provides excellent weather resistance for all-season hunting.

#3

Remington 783

$350 - $450 ★★★★ 4.2/5

Solid accuracy and classic design at an unbeatable entry-level price.

Pros

  • Most affordable option
  • Excellent factory trigger
  • Proven Remington 700-style action
  • Good accuracy potential

Cons

  • Quality control inconsistencies
  • Basic features compared to competitors
The Remington 783 brings the proven Remington 700 action design to an entry-level price point, featuring the same two-lug bolt design that has dominated American hunting for decades. The rifle includes Remington's CrossFire trigger system, which provides a surprisingly crisp 3.5-pound pull right out of the box. The 22-inch button-rifled barrel is button-rifled and properly crowned, contributing to consistent accuracy that typically runs 1.5-2 inches at 100 yards with quality ammunition.

While the 783 lacks some of the refinements found in pricier competitors, it delivers solid hunting performance at the lowest price point in our comparison. The black synthetic stock features molded-in checkering and sling swivel studs, while the SuperCell recoil pad effectively manages .308 recoil. The detachable magazine system works reliably, though the magazine release requires more deliberate pressure than some alternatives. Build quality has improved significantly since the rifle's introduction, though buyers should still inspect their rifle carefully. For budget-conscious hunters who prioritize proven action design and acceptable accuracy, the 783 represents excellent value.
